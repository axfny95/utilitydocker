## Every Percentage Calculation in One Place

Percentages come up constantly in daily life: calculating discounts while shopping, figuring out tips at restaurants, understanding interest rates, analyzing data trends, or grading exams. Our free percentage calculator handles every common scenario so you never need to fumble with formulas again.

## Types of Calculations Supported

### Percentage of a Number
The most basic calculation: what is X% of Y? Enter the percentage and the number to get an instant answer. Perfect for calculating sales tax, discounts, commissions, and proportions.

### Percentage Change
Determine how much a value has increased or decreased in percentage terms. Enter the original value and the new value to see the percentage change. This is widely used in finance, analytics, and reporting.

### What Percent Is X of Y?
Find what percentage one number represents of another. For example, if you scored 42 out of 50 on a test, this calculation tells you that is 84%. Essential for grading, statistics, and progress tracking.

### Percentage Increase and Decrease
Calculate the result of increasing or decreasing a number by a given percentage. Start with a base value, apply a percentage increase or decrease, and see the final result.

## Real-World Applications

**Shopping and discounts**: When a store advertises 35% off a $120 item, quickly calculate that the discount is $42 and the sale price is $78.

**Finance and investing**: Track portfolio performance by calculating percentage returns. If your investment went from $10,000 to $12,500, that is a 25% gain.

**Grades and scores**: Convert raw scores to percentages for academic grading. A score of 78 out of 90 is approximately 86.7%.

**Business metrics**: Calculate growth rates, profit margins, conversion rates, and year-over-year changes, which are all fundamental business metrics expressed as percentages.

## The Math Behind Percentages

The word "percent" comes from the Latin "per centum," meaning "by the hundred." A percentage is simply a fraction with 100 as the denominator. Understanding this makes any percentage calculation straightforward: 25% is 25/100, which equals 0.25. Multiply any number by 0.25 to find 25% of it. This tool automates that process for every type of percentage operation.
