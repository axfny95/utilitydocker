## Turn Your Images Into PDF Documents Instantly

Need to submit scanned documents, compile photos into a single file, or create a printable image collection? Our free JPG to PDF converter transforms your images into professional PDF documents entirely within your browser. No software installation, no account signup, and no file uploads to external servers.

## Why Convert Images to PDF?

PDF is the universal document format. While images can be viewed on most devices, PDFs offer advantages that make them the preferred format for sharing and archiving:

- **Consistent layout**: PDFs look the same on every device and operating system
- **Multi-page support**: Combine dozens of images into a single, organized file
- **Professional appearance**: PDFs are the standard for business documents, proposals, and submissions
- **Print-ready**: PDFs maintain exact dimensions and resolution when printed
- **Compact size**: PDF compression can reduce total file size compared to sending images individually

## Common Use Cases

**Scanning and document submission**: Scanned receipts, contracts, and forms are often saved as images. Converting them to PDF creates a professional, multi-page document suitable for email attachments or upload portals.

**Photography portfolios**: Compile your best photos into a single PDF that clients can easily browse and print. Each image becomes its own page with clean formatting.

**Academic submissions**: Many educational institutions require assignments in PDF format. Convert photos of handwritten work or diagrams into a single PDF for submission.

**Real estate and insurance**: Document property conditions, damage, or inventory by photographing items and compiling the images into an organized PDF report.

## Complete Privacy by Design

Unlike most online converters that upload your files to remote servers for processing, this tool does everything locally. The conversion uses client-side JavaScript libraries to generate the PDF directly in your browser. Your images are read from your device's memory, processed into a PDF, and the result is saved to your downloads folder. At no point do your files travel over the internet.

## Tips for Best Results

For the sharpest results, use high-resolution source images. If your images are photographs, JPG format typically produces smaller PDFs. For screenshots, diagrams, or images with text, PNG format preserves sharper edges and text clarity in the final PDF.
