## Calculate the Exact Number of Days Between Any Two Dates

Counting days on a calendar is tedious and error-prone, especially across months with different lengths. This date calculator does the math for you in milliseconds. Enter two dates and see the precise difference in days, weeks, months, and years, or add a specific number of days to any date to find the resulting calendar date.

## Two Modes, One Tool

### Date Difference

Pick any two dates from the past, present, or future. The calculator returns:

- **Total days** between the dates
- **Weeks and remaining days** for a weekly perspective
- **Months and remaining days** for a monthly view
- **Years, months, and days** for long-range durations

This mode is ideal for calculating project timelines, counting down to events, measuring the length of a lease, or figuring out how many days remain until a deadline.

### Add or Subtract Days

Start with any calendar date and add or subtract a number of days. The result includes the full date and the day of the week. This mode helps when you need to answer questions like "what date is 90 days from today?" or "what was the date 45 days ago?"

## Where Date Calculations Come in Handy

**Contract deadlines**: Legal agreements often specify durations in calendar days. Enter the signing date and the number of days to find the exact deadline.

**Travel planning**: Count the days between departure and return to estimate accommodation costs, check visa duration limits, or plan daily itineraries.

**Health and fitness**: Many workout programs run for a set number of days. Enter the start date and the program length to see your completion date.

**Academic schedules**: Calculate the number of school days in a semester, find midpoint dates, or count the days until graduation.

## Leap Year and Calendar Accuracy

The calculator follows the Gregorian calendar, which is the standard civil calendar used worldwide. Leap years occur every four years, except for century years not divisible by 400. This means the year 2000 was a leap year, but 1900 was not. Every calculation respects these rules, so your results are always accurate.

## Business Days vs. Calendar Days

This tool counts calendar days by default, which includes weekends and holidays. If you need business-day calculations, remember that a rough estimate is to multiply calendar weeks by five. For precise business-day counts that exclude specific holidays, a dedicated business-day calculator is the better choice.
