## Measure and Improve Your Typing Speed

Whether you are a student, a developer, a writer, or an office worker, typing speed directly affects your daily productivity. This free typing speed test measures your words per minute and accuracy with real-time feedback, helping you identify weaknesses and track progress over time.

## How the Test Works

A block of text is displayed on screen. Your job is to retype it as quickly and accurately as you can. The timer starts automatically when you begin typing and stops when you finish the passage or the time limit expires. Your results include:

- **Words per minute (WPM)**: The standard measure of typing speed
- **Accuracy percentage**: The ratio of correct keystrokes to total keystrokes
- **Error count**: The number of incorrect characters typed
- **Time elapsed**: How long the test took

## Why Typing Speed Matters

Studies show that proficient typists spend significantly less time on routine tasks like writing emails, taking notes, and entering data. A jump from 40 WPM to 70 WPM can save you hours every week if your work involves substantial text input. Faster typing also reduces the cognitive gap between thinking and writing, which can improve the quality of your output.

## Tips to Type Faster

**Use all ten fingers.** Touch typing, where each finger is responsible for a specific set of keys, is the foundation of fast and accurate typing. Hunt-and-peck methods create a hard speed ceiling.

**Keep your eyes on the screen.** Looking at the keyboard breaks your rhythm and slows you down. If you must glance down occasionally, that is fine, but make it a goal to eliminate the habit.

**Practice consistently.** Short daily sessions of five to ten minutes are more effective than occasional marathon sessions. Muscle memory builds through repetition over time.

**Prioritize accuracy over speed.** Correcting errors costs more time than typing slightly slower. Once your accuracy is consistently above 95 percent, shift your focus toward speed.
