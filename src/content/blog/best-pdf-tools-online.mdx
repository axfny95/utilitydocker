## Detailed Tool Reviews

### Smallpdf

Smallpdf offers a polished interface and supports a wide range of conversions (Word to PDF, Excel to PDF, PowerPoint to PDF, and more). The main limitation is the free tier: you get just two tasks per day. After that, you either wait 24 hours or subscribe. For occasional use, it is perfectly functional. For regular work, the daily limit becomes a bottleneck quickly.

### iLovePDF

iLovePDF is another well-known option with a broad feature set. It covers merging, splitting, compressing, converting, and even basic editing. Free users face file size limits and a cap on the number of tasks per session. The tool processes files on its servers, so privacy-sensitive documents should be handled elsewhere.

### PDF24

PDF24 is generous with its free tier — there are no task limits for most operations. It supports an impressive number of conversions and includes a basic PDF editor. The trade-off is a more cluttered interface and server-side processing for all operations.

### Sejda

Sejda provides a clean interface and strong editing capabilities, including the ability to add text, images, and signatures to PDFs. The free tier limits you to three tasks per hour and files up to 50 MB. It is a solid choice for light editing but not ideal for heavy batch work.

## How to Choose the Right PDF Tool

### Consider Privacy First

If your documents contain sensitive information — financial data, legal contracts, personal records — use a tool that processes files locally. The [PDF Merger](/tools/pdf-merger) and [PDF Splitter](/tools/pdf-splitter) on UtilityDocker both run client-side, meaning your files stay on your device at all times.

### Think About Volume

Free tiers on server-based tools typically restrict how many operations you can perform per day or hour. If you regularly process large batches, a client-side tool with no limits saves both time and money.

### Check Format Support

If you need to convert between many formats (Word, Excel, PowerPoint), a tool like Smallpdf or iLovePDF offers broader format support. If your primary need is merging, splitting, or converting images to PDF, a focused tool often does the job better and faster.

## Tips for Working with PDFs

- **Compress before sharing.** Large PDFs are slow to email and download. Compress them after merging to keep file sizes manageable.
- **Use descriptive file names.** When merging, name your output file clearly (e.g., "Q1-2026-Invoices-Combined.pdf") so you can find it later.
- **Check page order after merging.** Always scroll through your merged PDF to verify that pages are in the correct sequence.
- **Prefer vector-based PDFs.** If you are creating PDFs from scratch, use vector graphics and real text rather than rasterized images. The files will be smaller and the text will remain searchable.
