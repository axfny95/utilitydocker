Meta tags are HTML elements that provide information about your webpage to search engines and social platforms. While hundreds of meta tags exist, only a handful actually impact your SEO and click-through rates.

## The Meta Tags That Matter

**Title tag** is the single most important on-page SEO element. It appears in search results, browser tabs, and social shares. Keep it under 60 characters, front-load your target keyword, and make it compelling enough to click.

**Meta description** doesn't directly affect rankings, but it heavily influences click-through rates. Google displays it in search results as the page summary. Keep it under 155 characters, include your target keyword naturally, and write it as a call to action.

**Robots meta tag** tells search engines whether to index a page and follow its links. The default behavior is `index, follow` — you only need this tag when you want to deviate, such as `noindex` for admin pages or staging environments.

## Open Graph Tags

Open Graph (OG) tags control how your page appears when shared on Facebook, LinkedIn, Discord, and most other platforms. The essential tags: `og:title`, `og:description`, `og:image` (1200x630px recommended), `og:url`, and `og:type`.

A compelling OG image can dramatically increase social engagement. Pages with custom OG images get 2-3x more clicks than those using auto-generated previews.

## Twitter Card Tags

Twitter (X) has its own meta tags that override Open Graph when present. Use `twitter:card` (set to `summary_large_image` for maximum visibility), `twitter:title`, `twitter:description`, and `twitter:image`. Add `twitter:site` with your handle for brand attribution.

## Common Mistakes

1. **Duplicate title tags** across pages — every page needs a unique title
2. **Keyword stuffing** in meta descriptions — write for humans, not bots
3. **Missing OG images** — social shares without images get dramatically less engagement
4. **Forgetting canonical tags** — if content exists at multiple URLs, use `link rel="canonical"` to specify the primary version
